What Clay actually does (and why alternatives differ)

"Clay alternative" means different things to different teams. Clay is a workflow builder — you bring lists, configure enrichment columns across marketplace providers, run AI research, and push data to CRMs and sending tools. Not every alternative replaces that entire stack.

  • Workflow builders (Bitscale, Persana) — closest to Clay; spreadsheet grids, waterfalls, integrations.
  • All-in-one platforms (Apollo) — built-in database + sequences; less custom logic.
  • Enrichment APIs (FullEnrich) — email/phone finding only; pair with your own stack.
  • Enterprise databases (ZoomInfo, Cognism) — coverage and compliance; not workflow engines.

Knowing which category you need prevents overpaying for the wrong shape of product.

Why teams are re-evaluating Clay in 2026

Clay's March 2026 pricing overhaul is the main trigger — not a lack of product quality.

Dual-currency pricing: Data Credits + Actions

Clay separates Data Credits (marketplace enrichment) from Actions (integrations, formulas, HTTP calls, CRM syncs). Self-serve plans include fixed Action allotments — 15,000 on Launch, 40,000 on Growth — and Actions do not roll over month to month.

Metered workflow steps

Steps that were previously "free" when using your own API keys can now consume Actions. High-volume outbound teams report burning through allotments faster than expected.

Failed lookups and variable bills

Waterfall enrichment may charge across multiple provider attempts even when no match is found. At scale, failed lookups can consume a meaningful share of credits — a recurring theme in operator forums.

When staying on Clay still makes sense

Claygent remains the benchmark for AI web research at scale. The template library and community (Clay University, shared tables) are unmatched. Teams with grandfathered pricing or a dedicated RevOps builder often find the flexibility worth the complexity. We are not recommending everyone leave Clay — only that the 2026 economics deserve a second look if your bill has become unpredictable.

Which tool fits your team?

Three common profiles we see when teams outgrow or reconsider Clay.

Solo founder or small SDR team

1–3 reps · <5k enrichments/mo

You need contacts, basic enrichment, and outbound fast — not a RevOps project. Setup time matters more than waterfall depth.

Likely fit: Apollo (free tier + sequences) or Clay Launch if you enjoy building workflows.

RevOps-led outbound team

5+ reps · 20k–100k enrichments/mo · HubSpot or Salesforce

You run multi-column enrichment, push to Instantly/Smartlead, and sync back to CRM. Predictable platform cost and playbook speed matter.

Likely fit: Bitscale or Clay (if usage costs stay predictable). See our Clay vs Bitscale breakdown.

Agency running client workflows

Multi-client · variable volume · margin-sensitive

Per-client templates, outbound handoff, and cost you can bake into retainers. Action metering and credit expiry hurt agency economics.

Likely fit: Bitscale or Apollo depending on workflow complexity — details in our agency guide.

Tool profiles in depth

Equal-weight summaries — pros and cons included for each. Order is alphabetical, not a ranking.

Apollo

All-in-one prospecting · Database + sequences + dialer

Apollo is the default when teams want one login for finding contacts, running sequences, and calling — without wiring a multi-tool stack. Enrichment depth is shallower than Clay or dedicated workflow builders, but the free tier and per-user pricing work well for small teams.

Strengths
  • Generous free tier
  • Built-in sequences and dialer
  • CRM sync on all paid plans
Trade-offs
  • No multi-provider waterfall
  • Per-user cost scales with headcount
  • Limited custom enrichment logic

Bitscale

Workflow builder · Closest functional match to Clay for RevOps outbound

Bitscale targets teams that want Clay-style grids — waterfall enrichment, AI research columns, CRM sync, push to sending tools — with subscription pricing and unlimited Actions on paid plans. Credit rollover (up to 3× monthly) addresses another common Clay complaint. HubSpot two-way sync requires Growth (~$314/mo annual); Salesforce requires Booster (~$719/mo). The template ecosystem is smaller than Clay's community, but 75+ playbooks cover common SDR motions.

Strengths
  • 100+ enrichment providers, waterfall logic
  • Unlimited Actions on paid plans
  • Playbooks reduce setup time
  • Native Instantly, Smartlead, Heyreach
Trade-offs
  • CRM tiers gated on higher plans
  • Smaller community vs Clay
  • Free tier is test-only (200 credits)

Cognism

Enterprise database · EMEA phone-verified focus

Cognism wins on GDPR-compliant sourcing and phone-verified mobile numbers (Diamond Data) for European outbound. It is not a workflow builder — pair it with enrichment or sending tools for a full stack.

Strengths
  • Strong EMEA coverage
  • Phone-verified mobiles
  • Compliance-first sourcing
Trade-offs
  • No Clay-like workflows
  • Enterprise pricing
  • Weaker US coverage vs ZoomInfo

FullEnrich

Enrichment API · Waterfall email and phone only

If you only need verified emails and mobiles — and already have list building and sending covered — FullEnrich runs waterfalls across 20+ providers at lower per-lookup cost than full platforms. No CRM UI, no AI research columns.

Strengths
  • Pure waterfall specialization
  • API-first, stack-agnostic
  • Lower cost for enrichment-only
Trade-offs
  • No workflow builder
  • Requires technical integration
  • No native CRM sync UI

Persana AI

Workflow builder · AI-native, newer entrant

Persana offers Clay-like spreadsheets with AI agents for research and personalization. Smaller enrichment network and integration library than Bitscale or Clay, but simpler pricing and an AI-first UX appeal to early adopters.

Strengths

ZoomInfo

Enterprise database · Intent, org charts, scoops

The incumbent for large sales orgs needing broad coverage, intent signals, and hierarchy data. Annual contracts typically start in five figures. Overkill for SMB outbound; right-sized for ABM at scale.

Strengths
  • Largest B2B database
  • Intent and scoops
  • Deep Salesforce integration
Trade-offs
  • Expensive contracts
  • No workflow builder
  • Stale data on niche ICPs

Switching from Clay: what to expect

Migration difficulty depends on how custom your Clay tables are.

  • Standard enrichment columns (email, phone, company firmographics) — export CSV, import into a new grid, apply playbooks. Usually days, not weeks.
  • Claygent research workflows — hardest to replicate. Budget time to rebuild logic in another AI column or stay on Clay for research-only tables.
  • CRM sync mappings — re-map field pushes; verify two-way sync tier on the new platform before committing.
  • Community templates — no direct import; recreate from documentation or vendor playbooks.

Most teams run a parallel test on 500–1,000 records before cutting over — comparing coverage, cost, and CRM sync reliability on real ICP data.

Glossary

Actions (Clay)
Metered currency for platform operations — integrations, formulas, HTTP calls, CRM syncs — separate from Data Credits.
Data Credits (Clay)
Currency for purchasing enrichment from Clay's third-party marketplace.
Waterfall enrichment
Querying multiple data providers in sequence until a match is found — maximizes coverage vs single-source databases.
Playbook
Pre-built workflow template (enrichment columns, integrations) you clone and customize — reduces setup time vs building from scratch.
Two-way CRM sync
Pull records from CRM, enrich, push changed fields back automatically — no manual CSV export/import.

Frequently asked questions

  • What is the best Clay alternative?

    It depends on category. Apollo for all-in-one simplicity. Cognism for EMEA. ZoomInfo for enterprise ABM. FullEnrich for enrichment-only. For a Clay-like workflow builder, Bitscale and Persana are the closest matches — see our Clay vs Bitscale comparison for the most common fork in the road.

  • Is Bitscale just a cheaper Clay?

    Not exactly. Bitscale overlaps on spreadsheet workflows and waterfall enrichment but trades Clay's template depth and Claygent research for predictable Actions pricing and faster playbook-based setup. Some teams use both — Clay for research-heavy tables, Bitscale for high-volume enrichment and CRM sync.

  • Why are teams leaving Clay in 2026?

    Primarily cost predictability — metered Actions, non-rolling allotments, and failed-lookup charges. Product quality is not the main complaint. Teams with legacy pricing or low Action consumption often stay.

  • Can Apollo replace Clay?

    Only for basic motions. Apollo replaces Clay's database + sequencing layer, not custom multi-provider waterfalls or AI research workflows. See Clay vs Apollo.

  • Do alternatives support waterfall enrichment?

    Workflow builders (Clay, Bitscale, Persana) and APIs (FullEnrich) do. All-in-one databases (Apollo, ZoomInfo) rely on internal data instead.

  • How do I test before switching?

    Export a representative slice of your Clay table (500–1,000 rows), run it through a candidate tool's enrichment workflow, and compare field coverage, cost, and CRM sync. Most platforms offer free or trial tiers for this.
