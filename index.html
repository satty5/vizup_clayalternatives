Why teams are looking for Clay alternatives

Clay's March 2026 pricing overhaul reshaped how teams budget for enrichment workflows. Understanding the shift helps explain why alternatives are gaining traction.

Dual-currency pricing: Data Credits + Actions

Clay now separates Data Credits (marketplace enrichment from third-party providers) from Actions (platform operations: integrations, formulas, HTTP API calls, CRM syncs). Self-serve plans include a fixed monthly Action allotment — 15,000 on Launch, 40,000 on Growth — and Actions do not roll over.

Metered workflow steps add up fast

Every enrichment column, integration push, and formula run can consume Actions. Teams that previously used their own API keys without platform fees now pay per workflow step. For high-volume outbound motions, 15,000 Actions can exhaust quickly.

Failed lookups still cost credits

When Clay waterfall-queries multiple providers for an email and none match, you may still pay for each attempt. At scale, failed lookups can consume 20–30% of a team's credit allocation — a common complaint in operator communities.

When Clay still wins

Clay's template library, Claygent AI research agent, and mature community remain unmatched for teams that want maximum workflow depth and are willing to invest in learning the platform. If AI-driven web research at scale is core to your motion, Clay is still the benchmark.

Frequently asked questions

  • What is the best Clay alternative for GTM teams?

    It depends on your priority. For workflow-first outbound with predictable pricing, Bitscale is the strongest Clay-like alternative. For all-in-one prospecting, Apollo. For EMEA compliance, Cognism. For enterprise ABM, ZoomInfo.

  • Why are teams switching away from Clay in 2026?

    Clay's new Actions system meters platform usage separately from data credits. Actions don't roll over, HTTP API access is gated, and failed enrichment lookups still consume credits. Teams with high-volume workflows report less predictable bills.

  • Is Bitscale cheaper than Clay?

    For most RevOps teams, yes — especially when factoring in unlimited Actions on Bitscale paid plans vs Clay's metered Actions. Exact savings depend on volume and which integrations you need. Bitscale's Growth plan (~$314/mo annual) includes HubSpot 2-way sync; Clay's Growth is ~$495/mo plus usage.

  • Can I migrate Clay workflows to Bitscale?

    Bitscale offers 75+ pre-built playbooks that cover common enrichment and outbound motions. Complex custom Clay workflows may need rebuilding, but the spreadsheet-style interface makes migration straightforward for most teams.

  • Do Clay alternatives support waterfall enrichment?

    Yes. Bitscale, Clay, and tools like FullEnrich all support waterfall logic — querying multiple providers in sequence until a match is found. Apollo and ZoomInfo use internal databases rather than multi-provider waterfalls.
