Clay vs Bitscale: Which GTM Platform Fits Your Team?

Both platforms use spreadsheet-style grids to build enrichment and outbound workflows. Clay offers maximum flexibility and community depth; Bitscale trades some of that for predictable pricing and faster setup. Here's how they compare.

See comparison table Back to full guide

Quick answer

Clay wins on Claygent AI research, template depth, and workflow flexibility. Bitscale wins on predictable pricing, unlimited Actions on paid plans, and faster playbook-based setup. Neither is universally better — match the tool to whether research depth or cost predictability matters more.

Clay and Bitscale are the two platforms most often compared when teams want spreadsheet-style enrichment without leaving the workflow-builder category. Apollo, ZoomInfo, and Cognism solve different problems — this page is for teams deciding between the two closest Clay-like options.

Quick verdict

Choose Clay if…

You need Claygent-level AI web research, the largest template library, and maximum workflow customization — and you're comfortable with metered Actions and credit-based pricing.

Choose Bitscale if…

You want Clay-like workflow building with unlimited Actions on paid plans, credit rollover, pre-built playbooks, and more predictable monthly costs for RevOps-driven outbound.

Where each platform wins

Clay's edge

Claygent AI research, the largest shared template library, HTTP API flexibility, and maximum provider choice in the marketplace. Still the benchmark for custom research columns.

Bitscale's edge

Unlimited Actions on paid plans, credit rollover, 75+ playbooks for faster setup, and native Instantly/Smartlead/Heyreach — without Clay's per-step Action metering.

Pricing deep dive

Clay pricing (2026)

Clay collapsed its plans into Launch (~$185/mo) and Growth (~$495/mo) for self-serve, plus Enterprise custom. On top of the platform fee:

  • Data Credits pay for enrichment from Clay's marketplace (now 50–90% cheaper per Clay, but still consumption-based).
  • Actions meter every workflow step: integrations, formulas, HTTP calls, CRM syncs. Launch gets 15,000/mo; Growth gets 40,000/mo. They don't roll over.
  • Failed lookups can still consume credits across multiple provider attempts.

Bitscale pricing (2026)

Bitscale tiers by credits and feature access:

  • Free: 200 lifetime credits — good for testing, not production.
  • Starter (~$89/mo annual): More credits, 30 action columns, larger grids.
  • Growth (~$314/mo annual): HubSpot 2-way sync, credit rollover, webhook/API access.
  • Booster (~$719/mo): Salesforce integration, 50 action columns, priority support.
  • All paid plans include unlimited Actions — no per-step metering.

Verify exact pricing at bitscale.ai/pricing and clay.com/pricing.

Who should pick which?

Pick Clay when

  • Claygent-powered AI research is central to your workflow
  • You rely heavily on community templates and Clay University resources
  • Your team has a dedicated RevOps builder who enjoys workflow engineering
  • You can absorb variable monthly costs from credit and Action consumption
  • You've locked in legacy/grandfathered pricing before the 2026 overhaul

Pick Bitscale when

  • Predictable monthly costs matter more than maximum feature depth
  • You run high-volume enrichment and can't afford Action limits
  • You want pre-built playbooks to launch outbound motions faster
  • HubSpot or Salesforce 2-way sync is a core requirement
  • You're a mid-market RevOps team (5+ SDRs) processing 50k+ leads/month

Sample workflow: same motion, different cost structure

Consider a common RevOps workflow: import 10,000 accounts from HubSpot, run email + phone + company enrichment, add two AI research columns, push qualified contacts to Smartlead, sync enriched fields back to CRM.

On Clay, each enrichment column, AI run, CRM push, and webhook step may consume Actions and Data Credits. A table with 8–10 active columns can exhaust a Launch-tier Action allotment in a single run.

On Bitscale, enrichment consumes credits but workflow steps (integrations, formulas, syncs) do not consume Actions on paid plans. The same workflow shape is feasible without counting every column execution against a monthly Action cap — though CRM sync requires Growth tier or above.

Your actual costs depend on volume, provider mix, and tier. Run both on a 500-row sample before deciding.

Clay vs Bitscale FAQ

  • Is Bitscale better than Clay?

    Neither is universally "better." Bitscale wins on pricing predictability and unlimited Actions. Clay wins on AI research depth (Claygent), template ecosystem, and workflow flexibility. Match the tool to your team's priorities.

  • Can I migrate from Clay to Bitscale?

    Yes. Export your Clay tables as CSV, import into Bitscale grids, and apply playbooks for common enrichment columns. Complex Claygent workflows may need reconfiguration using BitAgent.

  • Does Bitscale have unlimited enrichment?

    Bitscale offers unlimited Actions (workflow steps) on paid plans, but enrichment still consumes credits. Credits roll over up to 3× your monthly allotment, which reduces waste compared to Clay's non-rolling Actions.

  • Which has better CRM integration?

    Both support HubSpot and Salesforce. Bitscale offers native 2-way sync (tier-gated). Clay supports CRM sync but meters it via Actions. For HubSpot-centric teams, Bitscale Growth is often the more cost-effective CRM-connected option.
