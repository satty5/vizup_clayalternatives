Pricing deep dive

Clay pricing (2026)

Clay collapsed its plans into Launch (~$185/mo) and Growth (~$495/mo) for self-serve, plus Enterprise custom. On top of the platform fee:

  • Data Credits pay for enrichment from Clay's marketplace (now 50–90% cheaper per Clay, but still consumption-based).
  • Actions meter every workflow step: integrations, formulas, HTTP calls, CRM syncs. Launch gets 15,000/mo; Growth gets 40,000/mo. They don't roll over.
  • Failed lookups can still consume credits across multiple provider attempts.

Bitscale pricing (2026)

Bitscale tiers by credits and feature access:

  • Free: 200 lifetime credits — good for testing, not production.
  • Starter (~$89/mo annual): More credits, 30 action columns, larger grids.
  • Growth (~$314/mo annual): HubSpot 2-way sync, credit rollover, webhook/API access.
  • Booster (~$719/mo): Salesforce integration, 50 action columns, priority support.
  • All paid plans include unlimited Actions — no per-step metering.

Verify exact pricing at bitscale.ai/pricing and clay.com/pricing.

Who should pick which?

  • You rely heavily on community templates and Clay University resources
  • Your team has a dedicated RevOps builder who enjoys workflow engineering
  • You can absorb variable monthly costs from credit and Action consumption
  • You've locked in legacy/grandfathered pricing before the 2026 overhaul

Pick Bitscale when

  • Predictable monthly costs matter more than maximum feature depth
  • You run high-volume enrichment and can't afford Action limits
  • You want pre-built playbooks to launch outbound motions faster
  • HubSpot or Salesforce 2-way sync is a core requirement

Clay vs Bitscale FAQ

  • Is Bitscale better than Clay?

    Neither is universally "better." Bitscale wins on pricing predictability and unlimited Actions. Clay wins on AI research depth (Claygent), template ecosystem, and workflow flexibility. Match the tool to your team's priorities.

  • Can I migrate from Clay to Bitscale?

    Yes. Export your Clay tables as CSV, import into Bitscale grids, and apply playbooks for common enrichment columns. Complex Claygent workflows may need reconfiguration using BitAgent.

  • Does Bitscale have unlimited enrichment?

    Bitscale offers unlimited Actions (workflow steps) on paid plans, but enrichment still consumes credits. Credits roll over up to 3× your monthly allotment, which reduces waste compared to Clay's non-rolling Actions.

  • Which has better CRM integration?

    Both support HubSpot and Salesforce. Bitscale offers native 2-way sync (tier-gated). Clay supports CRM sync but meters it via Actions. For HubSpot-centric teams, Bitscale Growth is often the more cost-effective CRM-connected option.
